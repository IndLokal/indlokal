# IndLokal — Pitch Playbook

_Version 1.0 — The spoken-word companion to the brand system._
_Owner: Founder & Brand. Companion to [`BRAND_GUIDELINES.md`](./BRAND_GUIDELINES.md), [`MARKETING_KIT.md`](./MARKETING_KIT.md)._

**What this is for.** What you **say out loud** — handshakes, panel intros, podcast interviews, founder coffees, partner meetings, LinkedIn voice notes — and the answers you give when someone pushes back.

**Why it exists.** When you meet a Consul General, a student, and a fellow founder in the same week, you should sound like the same company. The Marketing Kit handles the written surfaces; this playbook handles the spoken ones. **Same spine, same vocabulary, same three pillars.** They're separated because writing and speaking are different crafts.

**The discipline.** Memorise §1. Practise §2 out loud until the words come the same way every time. When in doubt, fall back to §1. If you find yourself improvising outside this playbook, stop and update _this file_ — consistency comes from repetition, not from filing.

---

## 1. The one sentence (memorise this)

> **IndLokal is the city-first guide to Indian life in Germany — the communities active near you, what's happening this week, and the practical stuff like Anmeldung and Kindergeld. All in one place, per city.**

That's the spine. Every pitch in §2 is a remix of this line. If you're ever stuck mid-conversation, fall back to it.

The three things this sentence carries — and that **every** version of the pitch must carry:

1. **City-first** — "in your German city", not "in Germany"
2. **Three pillars** — communities · events · resources (never drop one)
3. **All in one place, per city** — the consolidation promise

If a version drops any of these, it's wrong. Stop and rewind.

## 2. Pitches by length

### 2.1 — 10 seconds (handshake)

> "I'm building **IndLokal** — a city-first guide to Indian life in Germany. Communities, events, and the practical stuff like Anmeldung and Kindergeld, all in one place per city."

Stop. Wait for the next question. They will ask.

### 2.2 — 30 seconds (the version you'll use most)

> "There are hundreds of Indian communities in every major German city — Tamil sangams, founder groups, student associations, temple committees, cricket clubs. They all live inside WhatsApp, so unless you already know someone, you can't find them. Same with the practical stuff — Anmeldung, Kindergeld, the right Steuerberater. Every Indian relearns it from scratch.
>
> **IndLokal is the city-first guide to all of it.** Three things in one place, per city: the communities that are actually active, the events worth showing up to this week, and the resources every Indian in Germany ends up needing. A newcomer lands in Frankfurt on Monday, and by Friday they know who's around, what's on, and how to register at the Bürgeramt."

### 2.3 — 60 seconds (panel, podcast, partner coffee)

> "When I meet new Indians in Germany, they all describe the same first six months — lonely, confused, Googling _Anmeldung_ at midnight, joining six WhatsApp groups before they find one that's actually alive. I went through it. My friends went through it. Last week I met a Consul General who told me Indian life here is _a community of communities_ — and then asked me, _do you know how many Indian communities are active in Frankfurt? What's on this weekend?_ Neither of us could answer. The information exists. It's just unfindable.
>
> **That's what IndLokal fixes.** It's a city-first guide to Indian life in Germany. For each city, three pillars in one place: the **communities** actually active near you, the **events** worth knowing about this week, and the **resources** every Indian here ends up needing — Anmeldung, Kindergeld, EU Blue Card, GKV vs PKV, the consular camp dates, an English-speaking Hausarzt.
>
> It's activity-led, not a directory — ranked by what's actually alive. AI watches the sources, a human reviews everything before it goes live, so it stays fresh without an army. We send people _to_ WhatsApp, the event, the official form. We don't replace anything; we make it findable. Web for discovery, mobile app for the reminders that bring you back."

### 2.4 — 3 minutes (investor / serious partner)

A script outline, not a script. Same beats as the 60s — just slow down on each one.

**1. Hook (30s) — the Frankfurt question.** Tell the Consul General story exactly as it happened. Land the line: _the information exists, it's just unfindable._

**2. Three problems (45s).**

> "There are three problems stacked on each other. **Users** can't find what exists — newcomers especially, but even people who've been here years. **Communities** are invisible — a Tamil sangam with 800 active members has the same Google footprint as a dead Facebook page. And the **practical stuff** — Anmeldung, Kindergeld, EU Blue Card, doctors, Steuerberater — is locked inside Reddit threads and friends-of-friends. Every Indian arriving in Germany pays the same discovery tax, alone."

**3. What IndLokal is (60s).** The 30-second pitch (§2.2), slightly slower. Land the three pillars. Land _city-first_. Land _activity-led, not a directory_.

**4. Why us / why this is hard to copy (45s).**

> "Three things make this defensible. **Diaspora depth** — language, region, cultural filtering, plus a resource hub built around the questions Indians specifically ask. A generic expat board can't do that. **City-first density** — we don't expand until the launch city feels complete; you can't fake that with venture money. And **AI-assisted supply with human review** — that's what makes freshness affordable for a small team. Without it, the model doesn't work."

**5. Where we are and where this goes (30s).**

> "We've shipped the web product, the admin and organiser console, and the mobile MVP. Launching in the Stuttgart metro first. Funding plan is grants for the public-good layer — BAMF, EU AMIF, city integration funds — then B2B revenue from cities, universities and corporate HR who want a real channel into the diaspora, then a seed round for multi-city. Long term, this is the connective tissue between diaspora communities and the institutions that want to reach them. Indian-in-Germany first; the model is portable."

**6. The ask (15s).** Always end with one specific thing. Pick from §4.

## 5. When they push back — locked answers

Memorise these. Deliver them verbatim. If an answer here contradicts the product, fix the answer **first** — update this file and [`BRAND_GUIDELINES.md`](./BRAND_GUIDELINES.md).

- **"Isn't this just a directory?"** → "Directories are static. We're activity-led — events and freshness on top, communities and resources underneath. Ranked by what's actually happening, not who paid to be listed."
- **"How is this different from Meetup / WhatsApp / Facebook groups?"** → "Those are engagement surfaces. We're the discovery layer that makes them findable in one place. We send people _to_ WhatsApp — we don't replace it."
- **"How is this different from a generic expat platform?"** → "Diaspora depth. Language and regional filtering, communities they don't even know exist, and resources built around the questions Indians specifically ask — like CGI consular camp dates and Steuerberater who actually understand DTAA."
- **"How do you keep it fresh with a small team?"** → "AI watches the sources, extracts events and communities, a human reviews before it goes live. Freshness without scaling headcount."
- **"How will you make money?"** → "Grants fund the public-good layer. B2B — cities, universities, corporate HR — generates revenue. Users and communities are free, forever."
- **"Why Indian-in-Germany first?"** → "Fastest-growing diaspora, deepest fragmentation, strongest unmet need, and a corridor I know intimately. The model is portable later."
- **"Why a mobile app?"** → "Web brings discovery; mobile brings recall — push reminders for the events you care about. Without recall, people forget us."
- **"Are you replacing community groups?"** → "No. We make them findable and amplify them. The organiser is our partner, not our competitor."

## 6. Story bank — interchangeable proof points

Use **one** per pitch. Don't stack them. Update as the bank grows.

- **The Frankfurt question.** A Consul General couldn't tell us how many Indian communities are active in Frankfurt this week. If _he_ can't, no newcomer can.
- **The repeated discovery tax.** Every Indian arriving in Germany re-runs the same painful search — for community _and_ for paperwork. We pay that cost as a diaspora, over and over.
- **The invisible good community.** A regional association in {city} with hundreds of active members has no Google footprint; a dormant Facebook page outranks them. That's the gap.
- **The first weekend.** A student who landed Monday found three Indian community events and a Bürgeramt slot by Friday. That's the product working.
- **The collective voice / discovery layer line.** "You're building the collective voice; we're building the discovery layer underneath it." The line that makes other founders lean in.

## 7. Pre-meeting checklist (30 seconds before you walk in)

1. **Who is this for?** Audience door — §3.
2. **How long do I have?** 10s (§2.1) / 30s (§2.2) / 60s (§2.3) / 3 min (§2.4).
3. **One story.** Pick from §6.
4. **One ask.** Pick from §4.
5. **Spine check.** Does it carry **city-first**, **three pillars**, and **all in one place, per city**?

If yes — go. If no — fix it before you walk in.

## 8. The practice exercise that makes this stick

Tonight, before bed, say §2.2 (the 30-second pitch) out loud, three times. Tomorrow morning, do it again. Do that for a week.

After seven days, you will never deliver an inconsistent version again — because the words will come out the same way every time, no matter who's asking. **Consistency comes from repetition, not from filing.**
