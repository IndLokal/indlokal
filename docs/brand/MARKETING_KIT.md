> **What this kit is built around.** IndLokal is **not an events platform**. It is a city-first discovery layer with **three equally-important pillars**: the **Communities** active in your city, the **Events** they run this week, and the **Resources** every Indian in Germany ends up needing — Anmeldung, Kindergeld, EU Blue Card, GKV vs PKV, Steuererklärung, apartment search, English-friendly doctors, Indian grocers. Copy that talks only about events sells the product short.

## 2. Elevator pitches

### 30-second pitch (talks, intros, partner calls)

> Indian life in Germany is everywhere — and almost completely invisible. The communities live inside hundreds of WhatsApp groups, university societies, temple committees, language meet-ups, and weekend cricket clubs. The events are scattered across Eventbrite, Facebook, and posters nobody outside the group ever sees. And the practical stuff — Anmeldung, Kindergeld, the right Steuerberater, an English-friendly Hausarzt, the Indian grocer worth the train ride — is buried in 200-comment Reddit threads.
>
> **IndLokal is the city-first discovery layer for that hidden world.** For every German city, we map the active Indian communities, surface the events worth knowing about this week, and curate the consular and expat-life resources you actually need. Three pillars, one place, ranked by what's genuinely active — not by who paid for a directory listing. We don't replace WhatsApp; we're how you find the right group, the next event, and the answer to your question, in the first place.

### 60-second pitch (long-form, podcasts, press)

> There are over 250,000 Indians living in Germany, and the number is growing fast — Stuttgart alone draws hundreds of new arrivals every year through Bosch, Mercedes-Benz, Porsche, ZF and the broader automotive corridor. Most arrive into a city where they don't yet know a single person and a long list of practical questions. Where's the Diwali event? Which cricket club has openings? When is the next CGI consular camp in Mannheim? How does the EU Blue Card actually work? Where's a Hindi-speaking paediatrician? All of it exists. None of it is in one place.
>
> **IndLokal is the real-time guide to that world.** We do three things, equally well, for every German city we cover: we map the **communities** — Tamil Sangam, HSS, Malayalee Treffen, regional associations, professional networks, student groups — with activity signals that show you which ones are actually alive. We surface the **events** they run this week, ranked by freshness rather than recency-of-listing. And we curate the **resources** every Indian in Germany ends up needing — official guides to Anmeldung, EU Blue Card, Kindergeld, Steuererklärung, GKV vs PKV; CGI consular camp dates; Indian grocers, doctors, schools, driving licence conversion. We're the discovery layer, not an engagement layer — every successful interaction ends with a click out to the WhatsApp group, the event registration, or the official form.
>
> Available now at indlokal.de and indlokal.com. Launching in the Stuttgart metro — including Böblingen, Sindelfingen, Ludwigsburg, Esslingen, Göppingen — and expanding city by city across Germany.

## 13. Brand-safe vocabulary — words to use, words to avoid

### Words we use (warm, specific, plain)

community · communities · active · alive · this week · in your city · near you · find your people · show up · the right one · worth knowing about · plain-English · ranked by what's happening · curated · practical · trusted · the resources you actually need

### Words we avoid (vague, hype, exclusionary)

ecosystem · platform of platforms · revolutionising · disrupting · best-in-class · leveraging · seamless · synergy · world-class · _the_ destination for · #1 · _exclusive_ · _premium_

### Spelling and naming rules (also in `BRAND_GUIDELINES.md` §1)

| Use this                                                                                              | Don't use                                                                             |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `IndLokal`                                                                                            | `Indlokal`, `INDLOKAL`, `Ind Lokal`, `Ind-Lokal`, `IndLocal`                          |
| `indlokal.de`, `indlokal.com`, `@indlokal`                                                            | `indLokal.de`, `IndLokal.com`                                                         |
| `Anmeldung`, `Kindergeld`, `Steuererklärung`, `Bürgeramt`                                             | English translations in primary copy — these are the words people actually search for |
| `GKV` and `PKV` (with `Gesetzliche Krankenversicherung` / `Private Krankenversicherung` on first use) | "public/private health insurance" alone — loses the search term                       |
| `the Indian diaspora in Germany`                                                                      | "the Indian community" (singular — there isn't one)                                   |

This kit covers what we **write** (web, social, email, press). For what we **say** out loud — the 10s handshake, the 30s/60s/5-min pitches, audience-specific opening doors, the locked FAQ for hard questions, and the default ask per stakeholder — see [`PITCH_PLAYBOOK.md`](./PITCH_PLAYBOOK.md).

The two are aligned: same spine, same vocabulary, same three pillars. They're separated because written and spoken are different crafts and shouldn't share a file.
